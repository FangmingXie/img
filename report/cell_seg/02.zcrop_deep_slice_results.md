# Cell segmentation — dropping deep slices (Z-crop)

Date: 2026-06-26. Follow-up to `report/cell_seg/01.nuclei_segmentation_findings.md`.
Script: `scripts/cell_seg/02e.segment_nuclei_cellpose_zcrop.py`.
Voxel size: XY 57.5 nm, Z 160 nm (5.29×10⁻⁴ µm³/voxel).

## Motivation

Both prior 2D+stitch attempts struggled in the **deep slices**: the baseline (`02`)
missed dim deep nuclei, and the enhanced preprocessing (`02d`) amplified deep-slice
noise into tiny false masks (median crashed to 1–2 µm³). Rather than fight degraded
data, this experiment **drops the deep low-contrast tail** and reruns the *best*
procedure (the `02` baseline: per-Z percentile normalize → cpsam 2D + Z-stitch,
`cellprob=-1.0`, `stitch=0.4`) on the well-resolved slices only.

## Choosing the cutoff

Per-slice DAPI contrast (p99 / median) over the 39 slices:
- peaks at **z ≈ 18–24** (3.85–3.90),
- moderate at the top (z 0–2 ≈ 3.4–3.5),
- falls to a **low tail over z ≈ 27–38** (≈ 3.07–3.45, min at z=28).

→ Keep **z = 0–26** (27 slices); **drop the deepest 12** (z ≥ 27). The label volume
is padded back to full Z (dropped slices = 0) so the shared `03.measure_and_qc.py`
runs unchanged and the QC montage shows the dropped region as empty.

## Results

| Run | Slices | Count | Median µm³ | Mean µm³ | Max µm³ | Time |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| `02` baseline (full) | 39 | 50 | 7.09 | 15.78 | 101.1 | 5.3 s |
| `02d` enhanced (full) | 39 | 48 | 1.90 | 5.48 | 40.9 | 7.6 s |
| **`02e` zcrop (best proc.)** | **27** | **41** | **9.36** | 16.96 | 87.8 | 4.0 s |

**Biological target:** *Drosophila* nuclei ~2–4 µm across → ~4–14 µm³.

- **`02e` median 9.36 µm³ is the best yet** — squarely in range, and *higher* than the
  full-stack baseline (7.09) because the small-fragment / partial-nucleus tail from
  the deep slices is gone.
- QC overlays (`local_data/fig/cell_seg/03.qc_slices_zcrop.png`): clean single
  contours per nucleus on the kept slices; dropped slices correctly empty.
- A right-skew remains (mean 16.96 ≫ median; max 87.8 µm³) = a few **XY-merged
  doublets** — the one unresolved issue, independent of depth.

## Interpretation

Dropping the degraded deep slices is the most effective single change tried: it turns
the 2D+stitch result into a biologically defensible segmentation (41 nuclei, median
~9 µm³) at the cost of **not quantifying the bottom ~30 % of the stack**. For a field
where the deep tissue is genuinely unresolved, this is the right trade — better to
report a clean count over a defined depth than a noisy one over the whole volume.

## Caveats & next steps

- **Coverage:** counts/volumes now describe **z 0–26 only**. Report depth alongside
  the count. Nuclei centred in the dropped region are not counted (but those were the
  unreliable ones).
- **Cutoff is heuristic** (contrast-based, z≥27). If a manual count is available,
  re-tune the cutoff to it.
- **Residual XY merges** (max 87.8 µm³) — addressed by doublet splitting below.

## Doublet splitting — `02f` (post-hoc, intensity-guided)

`scripts/cell_seg/02f.split_doublets.py` splits oversized masks (> 18 µm³) with a
marker-controlled watershed *within each mask*. Key choice: **seed from peaks of the
heavily-smoothed DAPI** (σ≈6 px XY → ~one peak per nucleus), so cuts fall in the dim
valleys between nuclei. A first attempt seeding from the pure distance transform
produced **artificial straight-line cuts** and over-split big masses into 5–6 parts;
intensity-guided seeding fixed both. Sliver parts are merged into their largest neighbour.

| Run | Count | Median µm³ | Mean µm³ | Max µm³ |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| `02e` zcrop | 41 | 9.36 | 16.96 | 87.8 |
| **`02f` zcrop + split** | **52** | **10.95** | **13.37** | **51.3** |

Split 8 objects (into 2–3 parts each), including the 87.8 µm³ mass → 3 parts. Result:
**tightest distribution yet** (mean ≈ median, max halved), contours follow nucleus
boundaries (QC `local_data/fig/cell_seg/03.qc_slices_split.png`).

**Recommended default:** `02e` → `02f` (best procedure on z 0–26, then doublet split)
= **52 nuclei, median ~11 µm³ over z 0–26**. Still heuristic (no ground truth); a
manual count would let us tune the Z cutoff and the split threshold.
